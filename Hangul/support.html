Frequently Asked Questions

How do I restore my subscription?

Go to Profile > Settings > Subscription section. Your subscription is linked to your Apple ID and will restore automatically. If not, tap "Manage Subscription" or restart the app.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed through Apple. Go to iPhone Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions > Hangul Premium > Cancel. The subscription remains active until the end of the billing period.

Is the app available offline?

Yes. All learning content (975 words, 30 dialogues, quizzes, flashcards, handwriting, pronunciation rules) works offline. Only ads and subscription verification require an internet connection.

How do I change the display language?

The app follows your device language. Go to iPhone Settings > General > Language & Region to change it. The app supports English, Japanese, and Chinese (Simplified). All vocabulary includes translations in all three languages.

The pronunciation practice isn't working.

Make sure you've granted both microphone and speech recognition permissions. Go to iPhone Settings > Hangul and enable both "Microphone" and "Speech Recognition". If the issue persists, try restarting the app.

How do I reset my progress?

Delete and reinstall the app. All learning data is stored locally on your device and will be permanently removed when the app is deleted.

What content is free?

Free features include: all 40 Hangul letters with stroke order, syllable builder, 8 pronunciation rules, 8 word categories (400+ words), essential travel phrases, 3 dialogue scenarios, handwriting practice, daily quizzes (3/day), flashcard review (20/day), and daily goal tracking.

What does Premium include?

Premium unlocks: all 15 word categories (975 words), all 30 dialogues, unlimited quizzes (including Smart Quiz and TOPIK mock test), pronunciation practice, dictation practice, reading comprehension, unlimited flashcards, and ad-free experience.

How does the Smart Quiz work?

The Smart Quiz analyzes your quiz history to identify words you struggle with. It then prioritizes those words in future quizzes, so you practice what you need most.
